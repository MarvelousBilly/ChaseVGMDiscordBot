C	Refunct is an extremely short first-person platformer featuring tight movement tasking the player to push a series of buttons to reveal more platforms. The OST of the game comes completely from Singto Conley's album "Connections", which was created specifically for the game. C	It is 1913 and somewhere out on the Trans-Siberian railway, the Japanese army is transporting a young exorcist named Alice, after the horrific murder of her priest father in France. The army intend to use her for their own plans to seize control of China, but the train is intercepted by a warlock who attempts to kidnap Alice for his own occultist ritual. She is saved by the game’s protagonist, Yuri, an unhinged lone-wolf man capable of fusing with monsters, who is bound to follow a mysterious voice that commands him to protect Alice at all costs… Yuri and Alice flee across China, encountering grotesque creatures, curses and unusual characters along the way. They uncover some of the sinister forces at play in a dark pre-WW1 Asia and Europe, while also battling Yuri’s inner demons and malice. I found this game to be an overly-ambitious yet entrancing hot mess, that (while very flawed) has succeeded in inhabiting a corner of my brain long after completing it. This is in large part due to its creative, engaging battle system with its Judgement Ring, intriguing mysteries and setting, and of course its soundtrack! The story-telling aspect of the music is its greatest strength. Some parts use creepy voices and discordant sound effects (the main composer, Hirota, was the sound designer for the prequel horror game ‘Koudelka’); many tracks have distinct Asian music elements and traditional instruments like the beautiful title menu song ‘Sphere -qu-’; and others have an uplifting and big-open-blue-skies flavour, with signature Mitsuda basslines, like ‘Rice field of light’ and ‘Dalian’. There are easily another 20+ highlights in this soundtrack, so I tried to choose 5 that felt like a neat slice of Shadow Hearts’ gothic atmosphere.
